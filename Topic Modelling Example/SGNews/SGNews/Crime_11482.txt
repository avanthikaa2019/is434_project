The police have arrested eight men and one woman, aged 16 to 52, for suspected involvement in three separate loansharking cases.
In the first case, officers arrested 6 men and one woman at Jurong West Ave 5.
The five men and one woman had allegedly opened bank accounts and given away their Automated Teller Machine (ATM) cards and Personal Identification Numbers (PIN) to loansharking syndicates to facilitate their illicit business.
One of the men, aged 16, had assisted the loanshark syndicate to perform funds transfers via ATMs using his own bank account.
In the second case, police arrested a 24-year-old man along Dairy Farm Road. The suspect was believed to have carried out the business of moneylending without a licence.
In the third case, officers from Bedok Police Division arrested a 20-year-old man at Paya Lebar Road. Loanshark-related items, including a mobile phone and a notebook containing debtor's records were seized.
The suspect is believed to have carried out acts of harassment in Tanglin Halt Road and Haig Road by scrawling loanshark-related graffiti on walls.
First-time offenders found guilty of assisting in the carrying on of the business of an unlicensed moneylender may be fined between $30,000 and $300,000, jailed up to four years and caned up to six strokes.
First-time offenders found guilty of acting on behalf of an unlicensed moneylender, committing or attempting to commit any acts of harassment may be jailed for up to five years, fined between $5,000 and $50,000, and caned up to six strokes.
