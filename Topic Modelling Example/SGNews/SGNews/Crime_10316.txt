SINGAPORE - A 59-year-old man has been arrested by the police for alleged loansharking.
Officers arrested him in the Chai Chee Ave area on June 15 at about 11.30am after follow-up investigations into a case of loan shark harassment.
Investigations revealed that the suspect is a debtor-turned-runner who is believed to be responsible for cases of loan shark harassment in the Woodlands and Ang Mo Kio housing estates.
He allegedly splashed paint on the gates of debtors' residences and scrawled loan shark-related graffiti on the walls.
A handphone containing suspected debtors' records was found on him and seized.
He will be charged in court on Jun 16 under the Moneylenders Act 2010 (Revised Edition).
Those found guilty of loan shark harassmentcan be fined between $5,000 and $50,000, with mandatory imprisonment of up to five years and mandatory caning of up to six strokes.
Commander of Jurong Police Division Assistant Commissioner of Police Florence Chua praised her officers for their policing work.
