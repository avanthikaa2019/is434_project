SINGAPORE - The police have arrested a 47-year-old man believed to be responsible for several cases of snatch theft involving elderly victims around Upper Boon Keng Road.
Four cases of snatch thefts took place between Dec 6 2011 and April 8 this year. In all cases, the victims were lone elderly females who were wearing gold chains. The culprit would usually speak to the unsuspecting victims before forcibly snatching away their gold chains.
The suspect was arrested on April 17 when police officers spotted a man matching the victims' descriptions.
He will be charged in court on April 19 for an offence of snatch theft under the penal code which carries a minimum imprisonment term of one year and a maximum term of seven years. He may also be caned.
