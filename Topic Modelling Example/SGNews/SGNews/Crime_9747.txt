Police have arrested a 29-year-old man who is believed to be involved in a case of housebreaking.
On May 28 this year, a 20-year-old woman reported to the police that someone had climbed into her People Park Complex unit and stolen her mobile phone.
Police officers managed to establish the identity of the suspect and moved in to arrest him at Chinatown on June 21 at about 10.30 pm.
Preliminary investigations revealed that the suspect is believed to have climbed into the victim's unit through an unsecured window and stolen the victim's mobile phone, which was recovered.
The suspect will be charged in court on June 23.
If convicted, he faces a minimum imprisonment term of two years up to a maximum imprisonment term of 14 years.
The police advised residents not to leave their doors and windows unsecured, even when they are inside their premises.
