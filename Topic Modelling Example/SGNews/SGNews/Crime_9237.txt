Eight men and one woman have been arrested for their suspected involvements in loansharking activities.
The nine were arrested in an island-wide operation carried out from June 28 to 29.
Investigations revealed that the suspects are debtors who opened bank accounts and gave away thir ATM cards to facilitate the loansharks in their businesses.
Under the Moneylenders Act 2010 (Revised Edition), first-time offenders found guilty of assisting in the carrying on of the business of an unlicensed moneylender may be fined up to $300,000, jailed four years and given up to six strokes of the cane .
