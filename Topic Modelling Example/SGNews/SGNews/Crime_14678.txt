SINGAPORE - Police have arrested a two debtors-turned-runners, a 30-year-old man and a 19-year-old woman, for suspected involvement in loansharking activities.
In the first case on April 19 this year, officers from the Jurong Police Division were on patrol in the vicinity of Admiralty Place when they spotted a man performing multiple transactions at an Automated Teller Machine (ATM).
The suspect was found in possession of eight ATM cards, a stack of ATM transaction receipts, cash amounting to $800 and 3 mobile phone containing debtors' details.
Preliminary investigations indicate that the suspect is a debtor-turned-runner and was believed to have assisted the loanshark syndicate by checking for signs of harassment at debtors' residences, collecting ATM cards from other debtors and performing funds transfers via ATM at the loansharks' direction.
In the second case on April 20, officers from the Jurong Police Division arrested a 19-year-old woman for her involvement in several loanshark harassment cases.
Preliminary investigations revealed that the suspect is also a debtor-turned-runner. She is believed to have carried out harassment at debtors' residences in Jurong West and Sin Ming by splashing paint and scrawling loanshark-related graffiti on the walls. Two mobile phones containing debtors' information were also recovered.
The two suspects will be charged in court on April 21 for an offence under the Moneylenders Act (Revised Edition 2010).
First-time offenders found guilty of assisting in the business of unlicensed moneylending may be fined not less than $30,000 and not more $300,000, be imprisoned for a term not exceeding four years and shall also be liable to be punished with caning of not more than six strokes.
First-time offenders found guilty of loanshark harassment shall be fined not less than $5,000 and not more than $50,000 with mandatory imprisonment of up to five years and mandatory caning of up to six strokes.
