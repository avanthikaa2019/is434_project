The police have arrested a 20-year-old man for suspected involvement in loansharking activities.
He was seen splashing paint at a Hougang Ave 3 HDB unit by a resident on May 14 , who then called the police at around 4.30am.
Using the detailed description provided by the caller, the police located the suspected along Hougang Ave 2 and detained him after a short chase.
He was arrested after loan shark-related items such as bottles of paint, a marker, a mobile phone and a stack of papers containing suspected debtors' records were found in his possession.
The items were seized as case exhibits.
Preliminary investigations found that the suspect had committed several cases of loan shark harassment by splashing paint and scrawling loan shark-related graffiti on the walls of HDB blocks in Ang Mo Kio, Hougang, Jurong and Woodlands housing estates.
He will be charged in Court on May 15 for loan shark harassment.
Under the Moneylenders Act (Revised Edtiion 2010), those found guilty of loan shark harassment can be fined between $5,000 and $50,000, jailed up to five years and caned up to six strokes.
Commander of Ang Mo Kio Police Division Deputy Assistant Commissioner of Police Keok Tong San thanked the resident for his public-spiritedness and commended the officers for their policing work.
