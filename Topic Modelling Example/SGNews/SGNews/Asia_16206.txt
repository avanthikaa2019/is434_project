TOKYO - The transport ministry concluded Sunday that the rear section of a Japan Airlines plane scraped the runway while it was landing at Haneda Airport the previous day.
The Japan Transport Safety Board dispatched three inspectors to the airport Sunday to investigate the incident involving the JAL Boeing 777-200, which flew in from Shanghai and carried 308 passengers and crew members.
The inspectors examined the plane and questioned the pilot and others.
No injuries were reported in the incident.
According to the Land, Infrastructure, Transport and Tourism Ministry, scratches were visible on the rear section of the aircraft's body.
The pressure bulkhead, which is between the cabin and the tail, was slightly misshapen.
The incident took place at 4.08pm Saturday when the plane was making a second attempt to land at the airport in Tokyo.
