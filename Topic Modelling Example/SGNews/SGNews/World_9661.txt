MOSCOW - A 6.1 magnitude quake hit Russia's far eastern coast on Sunday, the US Geological Survey reported.
The quake struck at 11:15 am (0315 GMT) on the coast of the Kamchatka peninsula, about 580 kilometres (360 miles) north-northeast of the regional capital Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky, at a depth of 17 kilometres, the USGS said.
