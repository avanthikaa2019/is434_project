Police have arrested a 44-year-old man who used his previous residential address to obtain illegal loans from loansharks.
The current owners of that residential address became subjected to loanshark harrassment.
After follow-up investigations, the suspect was arrested by officers from Bedok Police Division around Tampines Street 21 on June 21, at about 4pm.
He is also being investigated for failing to report the change of his residential address under the National Registration Act.
Investigations in this case are still ongoing.
Under the Moneylenders Act 2010 (Revised Edition), any person who is guilty of providing false contact information to obtain loans from loansharks shall be liable upon conviction to imprisonment for a term not exceeding 12 months. In addition, under the National Registration Act, any person who is guilty of an offence of Failing to report a change of address shall be liable on conviction to a fine not exceeding $5,000 or to imprisonment for a term not exceeding five years or to both.
