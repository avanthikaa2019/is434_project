SINGAPORE - Police have arrested four men and one woman for their suspected involvement in loansharking activities in four separate cases.
In the first case, on June 21 at about 12.45pm, police officers arrested a 20-year-old woman in the vicinity of Lorong Melayu.
Investigations revealed that she is believed to be responsible for several cases of loanshark harassment held all over Singapore.
These loanshark harassment activities include those conducted in Dover Crescent, Woodlands, Choa Chu Kang, Bedok, Pasir Ris and Bukit Batok housing estates.
She allegedly splashed paint outside debtors' residences and scrawled loanshark-related graffiti on the walls.
Cans of paint and one mobile phone containing suspected debtors' information were seized in connection to the case.
In the second case, on the same day at about the same time, through follow-up investigations into cases of loansharking activities, another group of police officers arrested two men, aged 19 and 38, for assisting loansharking syndicates.
They allegedly opened bank accounts and gave away their ATM cards and Personal Identification Numbers (PINs) to facilitate illegal ATM transactions.
In the third case, at about 5.30pm on the same day, acting on a tip off, officers arrested two men, aged 29 and 45, for assisting the loansharking syndicates by procuring ATM cards from other debtors.
Upon their arrest, three mobile phones containing suspected debtors' information and one ATM card were seized.
The female suspect will be charged in court on June 23.
Investigations against the four male suspects are still ongoing.
First-time offenders found guilty of assisting in the business of an unlicensed moneylender may be fined up to $300,000, imprisoned for a term not exceeding four years and may also caned for up to six strokes.
First-time offenders found guilty of loanshark harassment can be fined up to $50,000 with mandatory imprisonment of up to five years and mandatory caning of up to six strokes.
