Police have arrested 19 people for suspected involvement in loansarking.
Twelve men and 7 women were arrested, one suspect even being as young as 15 years old.
Yesterday, an 8-hour islandwide operation commenced at 10pm at various locations and lasted till 6am the next morning.
Jurong West, Bukit Batok, Punggol, Bedok and Tampines were some of the locations involved in the simultaneous raids which resulted in the 19 arrests.
Preliminary investigations revealed that 17 of the suspects had opened bank accounts and gave away their ATM cards and Personal Identification Numbers (PINs) to loanshark syndicates to facilitate their unlicensed moneylending business.
The two remaining suspects consists of a woman and a 15-year-old boy.
The woman is believed to have obtained loans from loansharks by using her previous residential address.
While the 15-year-old boy is a suspected runner for an unlicensed moneylender by harassing debtors.
Investigations against the suspects are ongoing.
If found guilty of assisting in the carrying on of the business of an unlicensed moneylender, offenders may be fined between $30,000 and $300,000, jailed for a term of up to four years or be caned up to six strokes.
In addition, persons who borrow from loansharks using their old addresses are liable to be prosecuted for failing to report a change of residence, which carries a fine of up to $5000 and/or jailed for up to five years.
They are also liable for providing false contact information. If found guilty, they can be jailed for up to a year.
