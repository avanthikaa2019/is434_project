SINGAPORE - Four men aged between 30 and 36 were arrested by the police for allegedly stealing gold chains from a jewellery shop in Dunlop Street on May 30.
A sales woman, 58, was attending to a man in the shop when he suddenly snatched the seven chains she had in her hand and ran out of the shop. The chains are worth $53,000.
Her colleagues gave chase but were unable to catch him. They then made a police report.
On June 1, the shop staff found the men along Syed Alwi Road and detained him. Upon his arrest, three other men were caught in Changi Airport on June 2.
The three men who were attempting to flee the country had four gold chains in their possession.
If found guilty they can face up to seven years in jail and caning.
