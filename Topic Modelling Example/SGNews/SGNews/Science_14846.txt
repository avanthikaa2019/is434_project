Singapore's national infocomm competition will now be introduced at the primary school level to develop interest in the industry among students.
Acting Minister for Community Development, Youth and Sports Chan Chun Sing announced this at the annual National Infocomm Competition.
"Infocomm fresh graduates are highly sought (after), with more than 90 per cent from NUS, NTU and SMU obtaining employment within six months after completing their final examinations," said Mr Chan.
Separately, 19 students were also awarded the inaugural JC Computing Award.
These awardees be provided exposure to the infocomm industry through industry attachments and workshops.
