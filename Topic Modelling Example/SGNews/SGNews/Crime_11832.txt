SINGAPORE - A 41-year-old man suspected of involvement in loansharking activities has been arrested.
Police arrested the suspect in the vicinity of Ang Mo Kio Avenue 3 at about 11.55pm on Sunday.
Preliminary investigations indicated that the suspect is a debtor-turned runner who had assisted the loansharks in their businesses by performing electronic fund transactions.
Upon his arrest, several ATM cards were found in his possession and they were subsequently seized as case exhibits.
Investigations against the suspect are still on-going.
If found guilty of assisting in the carrying on of the business of an unlicensed moneylender, offenders may be fined between $30,000 and $300,000, jailed for a term of up to four years or be caned up to six strokes.
