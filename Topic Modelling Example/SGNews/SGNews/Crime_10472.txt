BANDAR SERI BEGAWAN - A police sting conducted by the Criminal Investigation Department, Anti-Vice Gambling Suppression Unit uncovered the immoral activities of two foreign females and a local man.
The arrest was made on June 12 at 9.06pm at a resthouse in the city's capital following a 'Letter of Authorisation' obtained from the Department of Community Development Department, Ministry of Culture, Youth and Sports under the "Women and Girls Protection Act, Cap 120" with all evidences seized for further investigation.
The Royal Brunei Police Force (RBPF) revealed that one of the foreign females was apprehended as she was allegedly soliciting customers and the other foreign female and male were caught in the act when personnel raided one of the rooms in the resthouse.
If found guilty the two foreign women will be charged under section 294A of the Penal Code, the first offence amounting to a fine not less than $500 and not exceeding $5,000 and imprisonment which may extend to one year, subsequent offences carry a fine of not less than $1,000 and not exceeding $10,000 and imprisonment which may extend to three years. Meanwhile the local man will be charged charge under section 294B of the Penal Code, the first offence carries a fine not less than $1,000 and not exceeding $5,000 and imprisonment which may extend to one year, with subsequent convictions carrying a fine of not less than $2,000 and not more than $10,000 and imprisonment which may extend to three years.
The RBPF would like to thank the public for the information and advised the public to jointly prevent immoral activities in the country.
