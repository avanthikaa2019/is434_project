SINGAPORE - Police have arrested a total of 33 men and women, aged between 15 and 65, in four separate cases for suspected involvement in loansharking activities.
In the first case, police conducted a 12h-long island-wide operation covering Kang Ching Road, Toa Payoh, Rivervale Drive, Bedok and Tampines housing estates on April 23-24. They arrested five men and six women.
The suspects are believed to have opened bank accounts and given away their Automated Teller Machine (ATM) cards along with the Personal Identification Numbers (PINs) to loansharking syndicates to facilitate in their illegal businesses.
In the second case on April 23, a 50-year-old man was arrested along with six ATM cards, two mobile phones containing bank accounts records and a stack of bank transaction receipts. The debtor-turned-runner is believed to have assisted the loanshark syndicates in performing electronic fund transactions via ATMs.
In the third case, two 19-year-olds were arrested along Pending Road on April 23. The police believe the duo was working with a 17-year-old accomplice, who was also arrested in the vicinity of Marine Crescent.
Preliminary investigations revealed that the trio were responsible for several cases of loanshark harassment in Woodlands, Bukit Panjang, Jurong East, Jurong West, Yishun, Hougang and Tanjong Pagar housing estates by scrawling loanshark-related graffiti on the walls and spraying paint at the targeted units.
In the fourth case, police made arrest of 18 suspects in the vicinities of North Bridge Road, Woodlands, Jurong West, and Clementi. They are believed assisted in illegal loansharking activities.
First-time offenders found guilty of assisting an unlicensed moneylender, may be fined up to $300,000, be jailed for up to four years or be caned up to six strokes.
For loanshark harassment cases, they could be fined up to $50,000 with mandatory imprisonment of up to five years and mandatory caning of up to six strokes, if convicted.
